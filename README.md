## The Big Idea Behind Cloudiux: Self-Managed vs. Hassle-Free

The first thing you'll notice when looking at their lineup is this split between **Self-Managed** and **Hassle-Free (Managed)** options. It's actually a pretty clean way to think about hosting:

- **Self-Managed**: You're the captain. You get the server, the specs, the power — and you deal with configurations yourself. Lower cost, more control.
- **Hassle-Free (Managed)**: Their team handles everything from A to Z, plus 24/7 live support is included. You pay more, but you never have to SSH into a server at 2am wondering why your site went down.

Neither is better universally. It depends entirely on whether you enjoy tinkering with servers or would rather never think about them.

## Virtual Dedicated Servers: Dedicated CPU Cores, Not Shared

This is the tier most people overlook, and honestly they shouldn't. Virtual Dedicated Servers (VDS) are different from standard VPS in one important way: the CPU cores are **dedicated**, not shared. On a regular VPS, your vCPUs are shared with other users on the same physical machine. On a VDS, those cores are exclusively yours.
